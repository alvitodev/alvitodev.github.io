## Overview

**NusaKultura** is an innovative educational mobile prototype application designed to explore and celebrate Indonesia's diverse cultural heritage. The project aims to enhance public knowledge about Indonesian traditions, arts, and customs while fostering deeper appreciation for the nation's rich cultural diversity.

Developed in collaboration with **INFINITE Research Group** at Universitas Negeri Yogyakarta, this project combines technology, education, and cultural preservation.

## Project Vision

To create an accessible, engaging platform that:

- Makes Indonesian cultural knowledge widely available
- Encourages younger generations to appreciate their heritage
- Documents and preserves endangered cultural practices
- Promotes cultural tourism and understanding

- **Traditional Arts**: Batik, wayang, puppet theater
- **Music & Dance**: Regional instruments, traditional performances
- **Cuisine**: Traditional recipes and cooking methods
- **Crafts**: Traditional handicrafts and techniques
- **Festivals**: Annual celebrations and ceremonies
- **Languages**: Linguistic diversity across regions
- **Architecture**: Traditional buildings and structures

## Design Approach

### User-Centric Design

The application was designed through extensive user research:

1. **Target Audience**: Students (ages 12-25), tourists, cultural enthusiasts
2. **Usability Testing**: Multiple rounds of user testing and feedback
3. **Accessibility**: Designed for various literacy levels
4. **Engagement**: Gamification elements to enhance learning

### Design Principles

- **Visual Heritage**: UI inspired by traditional Indonesian patterns
- **Clear Navigation**: Intuitive information architecture
- **Mobile-First**: Optimized for smartphone usage
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Respectful representation of traditions

## Research & Development

### Collaboration with INFINITE

The INFINITE Research Group brings expertise in:

- User research and validation
- Technical feasibility assessment
- Content curation and verification
- Prototype testing and iteration

### Key Research Findings

1. **User Need**: 87% of young users expressed interest in cultural learning through apps
2. **Content Preference**: Visual content (images/videos) rated as most engaging
3. **Feature Request**: Offline access and sharing capabilities highly desired
4. **Engagement**: Gamified learning increased user session duration by 3.2x
